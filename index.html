üîç Detective Work: Spot the Difference!

Look at these sentence pairs. What's different about them?

Pair 1:
A: "Shakespeare wrote Romeo and Juliet."
B: "Romeo and Juliet was written by Shakespeare."
Pair 2:
A: "The chef prepared the meal."
B: "The meal was prepared by the chef."
Pair 3:
A: "Scientists discovered a new planet."
B: "A new planet was discovered by scientists."

Great observation! You've spotted the difference between:

  • Active Voice (A sentences): The subject performs the action
  • Passive Voice (B sentences): The subject receives the action

Notice how the focus shifts from WHO does the action to WHAT receives the action!

üîß How to Build Passive Voice

Formula: Subject + BE (am/is/are/was/were) + Past Participle + (by + agent)

Active: The company builds cars.
Passive: Cars are built by the company.

Passive Voice Across Tenses:

  • Present Simple: is/are + past participle
    Example: "English is spoken worldwide."
  • Past Simple: was/were + past participle
    Example: "The letter was sent yesterday."
  • Present Perfect: has/have been + past participle
    Example: "The project has been completed."
  • Future Simple: will be + past participle
    Example: "The meeting will be held tomorrow."

üéØ When to Use Passive Voice

  1. When the action is more important than the doer:
    "The vaccine was developed in record time."
  2. When the doer is unknown or obvious:
    "My wallet was stolen." (We don't know by whom)
  3. In formal/academic writing:
    "The experiment was conducted under controlled conditions."
  4. To avoid responsibility or blame:
    "Mistakes were made." (instead of "I made mistakes.")
  5. For variety and flow in writing:
    To avoid repetitive sentence structures

üèõÔ∏è Museum Mystery: The Missing Artifact

Scenario: You're news reporters covering a story about a stolen artifact from a museum. Use passive voice to make your report sound professional and objective.

üì∞ News Report Template

Use these passive voice phrases:

  • was stolen / was taken
  • is believed to be
  • was last seen
  • has been reported
  • will be investigated

üì∞ Sample Report:

"A priceless ancient vase was stolen from the City Museum last night. The artifact is believed to be worth over $2 million and was last seen during yesterday's closing hours. The theft has been reported to local authorities, and the case will be investigated by specialized art crime detectives. Security footage is being reviewed, and witnesses are being interviewed."

‚úçÔ∏è Your Turn: Create Your News Report

Task: Work in groups of 3-4. Each group creates a 2-minute news report using passive voice. Include:

  • What was stolen
  • When it was discovered missing
  • What is being done about it
  • What will happen next

Remember: Focus on the events, not who is doing them!

üé§ Presentation Time

Each group presents their news report. Other students listen for correct use of passive voice and provide peer feedback.
