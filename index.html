📚 Lesson Overview

⏱️ Welcome & Introduction

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Master passive voice structure (be + past participle)
  • Practice converting active sentences to passive voice
  • Use passive voice in natural conversation
  • Apply passive voice in cultural and global contexts
  • Develop confidence in asking and answering questions using passive voice

📋 Lesson Structure

1. Reading Exercise (10 min): Japanese technology and culture passage with comprehension questions

2. Conversion Practice (10 min): Transform 15 Japanese sentences into English passive voice

3. Teacher Q&A (5 min): Answer teacher's passive voice questions

4. Student Q&A (5 min): Translate and ask questions in passive voice

5. Cultural Trivia (8 min): Explore world facts using passive voice

6. Wrap-up (2 min): Review and reflection

🔑 Key Grammar Point

Passive Voice Structure:

Subject + be + past participle + (by + agent)

Examples:

• The book is read by students.

• Sushi was invented in Japan.

• English is spoken worldwide.

📖 Reading Exercise

⏱️ 10 minutes

🏯 Japanese Innovation: From Tradition to Technology

Japan's remarkable transformation is admired by people worldwide. Traditional crafts are still preserved by skilled artisans, while cutting-edge technology is developed by innovative companies. The famous bullet train (Shinkansen) was introduced in 1964 and has been continuously improved since then.

Many Japanese traditions are respected and maintained across generations. Tea ceremony is performed with precise movements that have been passed down for centuries. Meanwhile, robots are being designed by Japanese engineers to assist with daily tasks and elderly care.

Japanese anime and manga are enjoyed by millions of fans globally. These cultural products are translated into dozens of languages and are watched in countries around the world. The influence of Japanese pop culture can be seen everywhere, from fashion trends that are inspired by Tokyo street style to video games that are played by people of all ages.

Environmental consciousness is also promoted by Japanese society. Recycling systems are implemented in every community, and energy-efficient technologies are constantly developed. The concept of "mottainai" (avoiding waste) is taught from childhood and is applied in both personal and business practices.

The bullet train was introduced in 1964.

Question 4:

Question 5:

Where is the concept of "mottainai" taught and applied?

The concept of "mottainai" is taught from childhood and is applied in both personal and business practices.

✏️ Conversion Exercise

⏱️ 10 minutes

📝 Instructions

Convert the following Japanese sentences into English passive voice using the structure: Subject + be + past participle + (by + agent)

彼らは新しいレストランを開きました。
A new restaurant was opened by them.
学生たちは宿題を完成させました。
The homework was completed by the students.
シェフは特別な料理を作りました。
A special dish was prepared by the chef.
観光客は美しい庭園を訪れました。
The beautiful garden was visited by tourists.
アーティストはこの絵を描きました。
This painting was created by the artist.
会社は新しい製品を発売します。
A new product will be launched by the company.
先生は学生に英語を教えています。
English is being taught to students by the teacher.
研究者たちは重要な発見をしました。
An important discovery was made by researchers.
彼女は美しい歌を歌いました。
A beautiful song was sung by her.
建築家は新しいビルを設計しました。
A new building was designed by the architect.
農家は新鮮な野菜を栽培しています。
Fresh vegetables are grown by farmers.
彼らは古い本を図書館で見つけました。
Old books were found in the library by them.
職人は伝統的な工芸品を作ります。
Traditional crafts are made by artisans.
子どもたちは公園で遊んでいます。
The park is being used by children for playing.
彼は重要なメッセージを送りました。
An important message was sent by him.

👩‍🏫 Teacher Questions

⏱️ 5 minutes

🎯 Instructions for Teacher

Ask these questions and encourage passive voice responses from your student.

Question 1:

Teacher asks: "What languages are spoken in your country?"

Sample response: "Japanese is spoken by most people in Japan, but English is also taught in schools and is used in international business."
Question 2:

Teacher asks: "How is traditional Japanese food prepared?"

Sample response: "Traditional Japanese food is prepared with fresh ingredients and is often served with rice. Sushi is made by skilled chefs and sashimi is cut very precisely."
Question 3:

Teacher asks: "What festivals are celebrated in Japan during spring?"

Sample response: "Cherry blossom festivals are celebrated throughout Japan in spring. Hanami parties are organized by families and friends under the blooming trees."
Question 4:

Teacher asks: "How are Japanese companies known internationally?"

Sample response: "Japanese companies are known for their quality products and innovation. Cars are manufactured by companies like Toyota and Honda, and electronics are produced by Sony and Panasonic."
Question 5:

Teacher asks: "What changes have been made to Japanese society in recent decades?"

Sample response: "Many changes have been made to Japanese society. Technology has been integrated into daily life, and more opportunities have been created for women in the workplace."
